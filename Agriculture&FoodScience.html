Agricultural Science

Agricultural sciences, sciences dealing with food and fibre production and processing.They include the technologies of soil cultivation, crop cultivation and harvesting, animal production, and the processing of plant and animal products for human consumption and use.

Food Science and Technology

Food science and technology is a multidisciplinary field that uses scientific knowledge and innovation to improve food processing and preservation for human consumption.

Courses

  1. B.Sc. in Food Science and Technology

    Duration: 3 years

    Eligibility:

    • needs to have studied Science at 12th/+2/PUC level or equivalent with physics, chemistry, and biology as the main subjects.

    • Some institutes may conduct their own entrance exams; others accept scores from national/state level exams like JEE Main, MHT CET, or university-specific exams.

  2. M.Sc. in Food Science and Technology

    Duration: 2 years

    Eligibility:

    • Bachelor’s degree in Food Science, Chemistry, Biochemistry, Agriculture, or related fields.

    • Entrance exams like GATE, ICAR AIEEA, or university-specific exams..

  3. B.Tech in Food Technology

    Duration: 4 years

    Eligibility:

    • needs to have studied Science at 12th/+2/PUC level or equivalent with physics, chemistry, and biology as the main subjects

    • JEE Main, JEE Advanced, state-level engineering entrance exams, or university-specific exams.

  4. M.Tech in Food Technology

    Duration: 2 years

    Eligibility:

    • B.Tech/B.E in Food Technology, Chemical Engineering, Biotechnology, or related fields.

    • GATE or university-specific entrance exams.

  5. Diploma in Food Technology

    Duration:1-3 years(varies)

    Eligibility:

Horticulture

Horticulture is the scientific study of agriculture and plant cultivation, including preparing soil for planting. Horticulture courses can include the study of plants for food, medicine, aesthetics, or ornamentals, as well as breeding techniques for vegetables, fruits, cereals, and other plants. Horticulture courses can lead to careers as horticulturists, plantation managers, floriculturists, and more.

Courses

  1. B.Sc. in Horticulture

    Duration: 3 to 4years

    Eligibility:

    • 10+2 with Science stream (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)

    • Some institutes conduct their own entrance exams; others accept scores from national/state level exams like ICAR AIEEA, EAMCET, or university-specific exams.

  2. M.Sc. in Horticulture

    Duration: 2 years

    Eligibility:

    • Bachelor’s degree in Horticulture, Agriculture, Botany, or related fields.

    • Entrance exams like ICAR AIEEA (PG), university-specific exams.

  3. Ph.D. in Horticulture

    Duration: 3 to 5years

    Eligibility:

    • M.Sc. in Horticulture or related field

    • Entrance exams conducted by universities or institutions like ICAR AICE-JRF/SRF (Ph.D.).

Salary Expectations

  • Entry-level: ₹2 to ₹ lakhs per annum

  • Mid-level: ₹3 to ₹4.5 lakhs per annum

  • Senior-level: ₹4.5 to ₹6 lakhs per annum

  • Top-level: ₹6 to ₹8.5+ lakhs per annum

Top Institutes

  • Indian Agricultural Research Institute (IARI), New Delhi

  • National Horticultural Research and Development Foundation (NHRDF), New Delhi
