Law

The Law program offers comprehensive education and training for individuals seeking to pursue careers in the legal field. The curriculum spans core legal subjects, specialized areas, and practical experiences to develop essential legal skills. Students gain expertise in constitutional law, contracts, property law, civil procedure, criminal law, and legal writing, with opportunities to specialize in areas such as corporate law, environmental law, human rights law, intellectual property law, and international law. Practical experiences such as moot court, internships, clinical work, and international moot courts enhance students' advocacy and legal research capabilities.

Courses

  1. Juris Doctor (J.D.)

    Duration: 3 years

    Eligibility:

    • A bachelor’s degree from an accredited institution.

    • Minimum GPA requirement, typically around 3.0 on a 4.0 scale.

    • LSAT (Law School Admission Test) scores.

  2. Master of Laws (LL.M.) in International Law

    Duration: 1 years

    Eligibility:

    • A first degree in law (LL.B. or equivalent) from an accredited institution.

    • Minimum GPA requirement, typically around 3.0 on a 4.0 scale.

    • A current resume or curriculum vitae.

    • Usually, two or three letters of recommendation.

  3. Certificate in Intellectual Property

    Duration: 1 years

    Eligibility:

    • Bachelor's degree from an accredited institution, preferably in law, science, engineering, or related fields. A Juris Doctor (J.D.) or equivalent law degree may be required for legal professionals.

    • Relevant work experience in law, intellectual property, or a related field may be required or preferred.

    • Completed application form, official transcripts, resume/CV, letters of recommendation, personal statement, and possibly an interview.

Criminal Justice

The degree in Criminal Justice provides a comprehensive overview of the criminal justice system, including law enforcement, the court system, and corrections. The curriculum includes courses in criminology, criminal law, forensic science, criminal procedure, and juvenile justice. Students will develop critical thinking and analytical skills, and gain practical experience through internships and fieldwork.

Courses

  1. Bachelor of Science in Criminal Justice

    Duration: 4 years

    Eligibility:

    • Completion of secondary education or equivalent (e.g., high school diploma).

    • Preferably with a strong academic record, particularly in subjects like social studies, history, and English.

  2. Master of Science in Criminology

    Duration: 2 years

    Eligibility:

    • A bachelor's degree from an accredited institution.Preferred fields of study include criminology, criminal justice, sociology, psychology, or related social sciences.

    • A minimum Grade Point Average (GPA) as required by the institution, typically around 3.0 on a 4.0 scale.

    • For non-native English speakers, proof of English language proficiency through tests such as TOEFL or IELTS is required.

    • Usually, two or three letters of recommendation from academic or professional references.

    • A current resume or curriculum vitae highlighting academic and professional achievements.

  3. Certificate in Forensic Science

    Duration: 1 years

    Eligibility:

    • Completion of secondary education or equivalent (e.g., high school diploma).

    • Minimum GPA requirement varies by program.

    • A written statement outlining interest in forensic science.

    • Preferred or required background in science or criminal justice

Salary Expectations

Public Policy

The Public Policy program prepares students at various educational levels to analyze, develop, and implement policies that address societal issues. The curriculum encompasses a broad range of subjects including political science, economics, public administration, policy analysis, ethics, statistics, and public management. Students learn to critically assess the impact of policies and develop strategic solutions for challenges in both the public and private sectors. Practical experiences such as internships, policy research projects, and capstone courses are integral components of the program, providing hands-on learning opportunities.

    • SAT or ACT scores, if required by the institution.

    • TOEFL or IELTS scores for non-native English speakers.

    • Usually, one or two letters of recommendation.

    • A written statement outlining interest in public policy.

    Duration: 2 years

    • A minimum Grade Point Average (GPA) as required by the institution, typically around 3.0 on a 4.0 scale.

    • For non-native English speakers, proof of English language proficiency through tests such as TOEFL or IELTS is required.

    • Usually, two or three letters of recommendation from academic or professional references.

    • A current resume or curriculum vitae highlighting academic and professional achievements.

  3. Certificate in Policy Analysis

    Duration: 1 years

    Eligibility:
